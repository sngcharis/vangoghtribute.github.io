Brief Timeline

  • March 30, 1853: Vincent van Gogh was born

    Van Gogh was born in Groot-Zundert, Netherlands.

  • 1864: Sent to Boarding School (1864 to 1866)

    At age 11, Van Gogh was sent to boarding school by his parents. Later in 1868, he returned home as he felt deeply unhappy and abandoned. He later wrote that his youth was "austere and cold, and sterile".

  • July, 1869: Employed by The Hague Gallery

    Van Gogh's uncle obtained a position for him at the art dealers. He was later transferred to London.

  • 1876: Moved to Borinage, Belgium

    Van Gogh begins devoting his life to the evangelization of the poor in the town of Borinage.

  • 1880: Moved to Brussels, Belgium

    Van Gogh relocates to Brussels and begins learning the skills needed to become an artist.

  • 1885: Completion of The Potato Eaters

    Van Gogh's first masterpiece, The Potato Eaters, was completed in Belgium.

  • March, 1886: Moved to Paris

    Van Gogh relocates to Paris. In Paris, he discovered the Impressionists and Post Impressionists artwork.

  • February, 1888: Moved to Arles

    Van Gogh relocates to Arles. His mental decline began relatively soon after the move. During this period in Arles, Van Gogh completed a large number of works, including the Sunflowers series.

  • December, 1888: Van Gogh severed part of his ear

    Van Gogh severed part of his left ear after an after an altercation with his friend. Van Gogh continually suffered from hallucinations and delusions.

  • May 8, 1889: Committed to Asylum

    Van Gogh voluntarily admitted himself to the Saint-Paul-de-Mausole asylum. During the year in asylum, Van Gogh completed roughly 142 paintings including The Starry Night, Wheatfield with Cypresses, Irises.

  • July 29, 1890: Death

    On 27 July 1890, aged 37, Van Gogh shot himself in the chest. He passed away two days later on 29 July in the arms of his brother, Theo.
